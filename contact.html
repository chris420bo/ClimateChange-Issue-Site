About 350.org

350.org is a global environmental organization that focuses on addressing climate change and promoting climate action. The organization's name, "350," refers to the safe upper limit of carbon dioxide (CO2) concentration in the Earth's atmosphere, measured in parts per million (ppm). Scientists widely agree that surpassing 350 ppm could lead to irreversible and dangerous climate impacts.

The organization was founded in 2008 by a group of environmentalists, including author Bill McKibben, to raise awareness about the importance of reducing greenhouse gas emissions and to push for policies that address climate change. They work to foster collaboration and coordination among diverse climate groups worldwide. They also support and amplify the efforts of local organizations and activists through partnerships, providing resources, and facilitating global campaigns.

What's the goal

The organization's mission is to build a global grassroots movement to solve the climate crisis and transition to a sustainable, clean energy future. They are dedicated to raising awareness about the dangers of surpassing 350 parts per million (ppm) of carbon dioxide in the Earth's atmosphere, a threshold beyond which irreversible and harmful climate impacts are likely to occur. They strive to mobilize individuals, communities, and institutions to take bold action against climate change.
